# SOP-006: pH Value Deviation in Solution

**Description:** This procedure outlines the corrective actions when the pH of a buffered solution or in-process liquid falls outside the required specification range.

**Action Steps:**
1. Stop the addition of any further ingredients to the batch. Keep the mixing vessel agitated.
2. Re-calibrate the pH meter using standard buffer solutions to ensure the reading is accurate.
3. Re-measure the pH. If it is still out of specification, notify the shift supervisor immediately.
4. Under supervision, make micro-adjustments using a pre-approved acid or base solution (e.g., 0.1M HCl or 0.1M NaOH) to bring the pH back into range.
5. Document the initial deviation, the recalibration, and all corrective additions in the batch record.
