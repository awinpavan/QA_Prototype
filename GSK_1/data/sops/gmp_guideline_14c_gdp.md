# GMP Guideline 14.C: Good Documentation Practices (GDP)

**Description:** This guideline establishes the standard for all manufacturing documentation. The principle is that "if it wasn't written down, it didn't happen."

**Core Principles:**
- All entries must be made in permanent, indelible black ink.
- No data or entries may be obscured or erased. Corrections must be made with a single line through the error, accompanied by the initials, date, and a reason for the change.
- All fields on a batch record must be completed. If a step is not applicable, it must be marked as "N/A".
- All documentation must be completed at the time the action is performed, not before or after.
